What makes a song sad?

Songs are sad becuase it is placed in the minor third key. Many muscisions have used this technique since the 17th century. The minor third's emotional steryotype is is closely related to the to the idea that western music are generlly upbeat, which are written in the major key. An example of this is like in the tune 'Happy Birthday'. Songs like Eleanor Rigby by the Beatles which are written in the minor key tend to be more sad. An example of a song that is written in a minor third key is Say Something by A Great Big World featuring Christina Aguilera. An exeption to this pattern is Let her Go by Passenger. The song is written in G major (G, Am, Bm, C, D, Em F#dim). G major can also be called an E minor. When it's used as a minor key, the minor key not is more 'stressed' than the major key note. However the same chords still apply. When you 'stress' a note is when an accent is placed upon it.


What is in a Minor Third? 

In a minor third there is one less semitone than there is a major interval. For an example, C to an E is a major third (4 half steps). A, C to an Eb is a minor 3rd (3 half steps). An example of this is C to E is a major third (4 semitones), C to Eb is a minor third (3 semitones).
