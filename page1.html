Is it the Lyrics or the Music that Effects your Mood?

The Studies

The fMRI Brain Activation Study:
A study done by Elvira Brattico in Germany and Finland, has looked at the fMRI brain activation. The study is when people listen to happy and sad music with and without lyrics. They didn't use the western classical music and use a wide range of musical genres, selected by 16 participants. The participants had a wide range of music training.

The Results: 

Acoustic Results:

Happy music with Happy music with lyrics had a faster attack on the slope and had the brightest timbres.

Happy music have brighter timbres and have faster tempos than sad music and are usually are in major mode.

Music and lyrics has brighter timbres than without music. 

 



fMRI analysis:

Sad Music with lyrics:

Sad music with lyrics have a unique activation in several parts of the brain that were not active when lyrics were not there, including parahippocampal gyrus, amgydala, medial and inferior frontal gyri (including Broca’s area) and the auditory cortex. 

Happy music with lyrics: 

Happy music with lyrics only had auditory responses alone. 

Happy music without lyrics:

The lymbic system and the the inferior frontal gyrus systems were activated. 




Effects of Melody and Lyrics on Mood and Memory:

137 undergraduate students  which attended to Le Moyne College volunteered in a study on music and its effects on mood and memory. There were 2 lyric conditions (happy and sad lyrics) and 3 music conditions (No music, happy instrumental and sad instrumental). Particpates were asked to listen to instrumental music or mentally make a melody while reading lyrics. The study tested memory, self-reported mood state and phycological arousal. The study displayed that people are influenced by the music played, not with lyrics. The results also displayed that participants that were not exposed to music they had a higher score on the memory test. Personal connectionsto lyrics did not connect to memory.
