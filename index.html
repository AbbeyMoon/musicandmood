Music and Mood

The main focus of my FYI (Follow Your Interest Project) this semester has been the relationship between music and mood. I have researched this project with the following aspects; English, Maths, technology, science and the arts. I have done this to have a full understanding of how your brain works in terms of emotions. 


Previously, I had already known the basics of how your mood changes. I had to research a range of Scientific questions highlighting a detailed description of the sequence of how your mood regulates and changes. I also had to research many studies that helped people understand the relationship of music and mood. I also researched the treatment, Music therapy to get a deeper understanding of how your brain reacts when you listen to music, whether it is lyrical or not.
After reading many articles of the process of your mood changing, I started to research my artistic aspect of my project. I looked at different songs and the techniques the have used to get a different result for each listener.

In order to know if music triggers the change in your mood or the lyrics i had to research different studies that showed the effects of lyrical music and ones without. This was my English aspect.

As I previously researched Music Therapy, I wanted to know more about how it could be easier access for people who were on the go or at home. I created a research questions about technology, which were on what the best apps that would have a better influence on people.
I was also interested if different languages had different results in mood. This was my research on the languages.

I hope to create a flipbook at the end of my project on how dopamine is released in the brain and be able to answer most questions on how your brain works in the emotion section.
